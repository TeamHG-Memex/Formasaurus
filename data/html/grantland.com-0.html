The Weight of Guilt: Inside the World of Competitive Bass Fishing Cheaters

by David Hill

In the spring of 1984, Danny Ray Davis caught an 8.7-pound bass in the KYKX Big Bass Classic. It wasn’t big enough to stuff and mount on the wall, but it was big enough to qualify for the $105,000 first prize. Winning two major bass fishing tournaments in less than a year? He was lucky. He was ecstatic. He was in deep shit.
